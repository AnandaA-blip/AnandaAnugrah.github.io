RFM Analysis: Customer Segmentation for Medical Sales

Not all customers are equal. This project applies RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis to a medical products distributor's sales data to segment customers by value, identify at-risk accounts, and recommend targeted retention strategies.

The Business Problem

A medical products distributor was treating all customers the same — equal marketing attention, equal outreach cadence, equal resources. This approach is inefficient: high-value customers may feel underserved while effort is wasted on dormant accounts. The business question was: Who are our most valuable customers? Who is at risk of churning? Which dormant accounts are worth re-engaging? RFM analysis provides a data-driven framework to answer all three.

Dataset

Historical sales transaction data from a medical products distributor, including customer IDs, transaction dates, product categories, and revenue per order. Data was processed using Python (pandas) to calculate the three RFM dimensions for each customer relative to a reference date. Looker Studio was used to build an interactive reporting dashboard.

Analysis Process

1

Data Preparation — Cleaned and standardized transaction records. Removed test accounts and incomplete orders. Calculated the analysis reference date (most recent transaction date in the dataset).

2

RFM Score Calculation — Computed three metrics per customer: Recency (days since last purchase), Frequency (total number of transactions), and Monetary Value (total revenue generated). Each metric was scored on a 1–5 scale using quintile ranking.

3

Customer Segmentation — Combined R, F, and M scores to assign each customer to a behavioral segment: Champions, Loyal Customers, At-Risk, Potential Loyalists, and Lost/Dormant. Segment rules were defined based on industry-standard RFM thresholds.

4

Segment Profiling — Calculated the average revenue, order frequency, and recency for each segment. Identified which product categories were most popular within high-value segments.

5

Dashboard & Recommendations — Built an interactive Looker Studio dashboard showing segment distribution, revenue concentration by segment, and a prioritized list of at-risk customers by account value.

Key Insights

Revenue is Concentrated in a Small Customer Group

The top 20% of customers (Champions and Loyal segments) contributed approximately 65–70% of total revenue — confirming a classic Pareto distribution in the customer base.

At-Risk Customers Represent Significant Recoverable Revenue

A segment of previously high-frequency customers showed declining recency, indicating early churn signals. Proactive outreach to this group has a higher ROI than acquiring new customers at equivalent revenue levels.

Dormant Accounts Require Qualification Before Re-Engagement

Many dormant accounts had low monetary value and low frequency, suggesting they were trial customers. Only a subset — those with historical high monetary value — are worth targeted re-engagement campaigns.

Business Impact

The RFM segmentation provides the sales and marketing team with a clear prioritization framework: protect Champions with premium service, re-engage At-Risk high-value accounts first, and deprioritize low-value dormant accounts. If retention of at-risk accounts is improved by 20%, the estimated revenue protection impact is material for a business of this scale.

Why This Project Stands Out

RFM analysis is a widely used, business-oriented segmentation method that directly connects to sales strategy — making it immediately understandable to non-technical stakeholders. The healthcare/medical context adds domain relevance. This project shows the ability to take a structured analytical method, apply it to real business data, and translate results into actionable recommendations — exactly what business analysts and healthcare data roles require.
